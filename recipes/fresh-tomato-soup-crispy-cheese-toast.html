Fresh Tomato Soup with Crispy Cheese Toast

Description

We're doing a very fresh twist on the classic cream of tomato soup with a grilled cheese sandwich. Since we're using fresh tomatoes at their peak of vine-ripened goodness, this simple soup is going to be not quite, but almost, 100% tomato.

Ingredients

  • 3 ½ pounds vine-ripened tomatoes
  • 4 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 anchovy fillet
  • 1 teaspoon salt, or to taste
  • 1 pinch red pepper flakes (Optional)
  • 8 slices Italian bread
  • ¼ cup thinly sliced basil leaves
  • 6 ounces shredded sharp white Cheddar cheese

Steps

  1. Remove cores from tomatoes and cut in half crosswise
  2. Heat olive oil and butter in a saucepan over medium-high heat
  3. Add garlic and anchovy and cook until garlic is sizzling in the hot fat, 45 seconds to 1 minute
  4. Carefully add the tomatoes with their juices, salt, and red pepper flakes
  5. Toss with a spoon every few minutes until the tomatoes release their liquid and start to break down
  6. Once the tomatoes have softened and start to collapse, reduce heat to medium-low and cook, stirring occasionally
  7. Preheat the oven to 450 degrees F
  8. Line a baking sheet with foil or a silicone liner. Drizzle with 2 tablespoons olive oil
  9. Place the bread slices on the prepared baking sheet. Top each slice with sliced basil, and then Cheddar cheese
  10. Bake in the preheated oven until the cheese is well browned and crispy around the edges, 15 to 20 minutes
  11. Remove soup from heat and pass through a mesh strainer into a bowl
  12. Use the back of a ladle to push all the tomato mixture through
  13. Discard the skins and seeds, which should be the only things left in the strainer
  14. Transfer the strained tomato soup back into the saucepan and place back over medium-low heat
  15. ontinue cooking to thicken slightly, if desired, or simply bring back to a simmer and serve immediately with the cheese toast
